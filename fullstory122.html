Story 122 – Non-verbal Communication (Negotiation) During the meeting, the manager sat with an open stance. He didn't slouch in his chair. This showed he was ready to listen. The client tapped his pen, suggesting he was nervous. The manager mirrored the client's movements to build rapport. A slight frown showed confusion. When the price was mentioned, the client raised his eyebrows. The manager kept his palms visible to show honesty. The silence was used as a powerful tool. They ended the deal with a firm handshake. Their expressions were positive. Understanding body language helped them succeed.

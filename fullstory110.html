Story 110 – Non-verbal Communication Skills The speaker stood tall with good posture. He maintained eye contact with the audience to show confidence. He didn't fidget with his hands. When the guest asked a question, the speaker nodded to show he was listening. A warm smile made the atmosphere feel friendly. His gestures were clear and purposeful. The student sat with crossed arms, which showed defensive body language. The teacher leaned forward to show interest. They communicated without speaking a word. A firm handshake ended the meeting. Non-verbal cues are as important as words. The audience clapped because they felt the speaker's energy.

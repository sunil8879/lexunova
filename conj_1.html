CONJUNCTIONS

PART I

1. Statement : The cat sat on the mat, and the dog slept by the fire.

conjunctions identification : and (Coordinating)

Explanation : "and" connects two independent clauses: "The cat sat on the mat" and "the dog slept by the fire."

2. Statement : He walked to the store, but it was closed.

conjunctions identification : but (Coordinating)

Explanation : "but" connects two independent clauses, showing a contrast: "He walked to the store" and "it was closed."

3. Statement : They are waiting because the bus is late.

conjunctions identification : because (Subordinating)

Explanation : "because" introduces a subordinate clause ("the bus is late") that explains the reason for the main clause ("They are waiting").

4. Statement : You can have tea or coffee.

conjunctions identification : or (Coordinating)

Explanation : "or" connects two nouns ("tea," "coffee"), offering a choice.

5. Statement : Although it was raining, we went for a walk.

conjunctions identification : Although (Subordinating)

Explanation : "Although" introduces a subordinate clause ("it was raining") that presents a contrast to the main clause ("we went for a walk").

6. Statement : The sun shines, so the day is bright.

conjunctions identification : so (Coordinating - result)

Explanation : "so" connects two independent clauses, showing a result: "The sun shines" and "the day is bright."

7. Statement : He reads books whenever he has free time.

conjunctions identification : whenever (Subordinating)

Explanation : "whenever" introduces a subordinate clause ("he has free time") that indicates the time of the action in the main clause ("He reads books").

8. Statement : The children played until it got dark.

conjunctions identification : until (Subordinating)

Explanation : "until" introduces a subordinate clause ("it got dark") that indicates the duration or end point of the action in the main clause ("The children played").

9. Statement : If you study, you will pass.

conjunctions identification : If (Subordinating)

Explanation : "If" introduces a subordinate clause ("you study") that states a condition for the main clause ("you will pass").
